rosemary lemon grilled chicken

This simple, sensational marinade and sauce for grilled chicken is made with lemon, garlic, rosemary, and butter. Separate the marinade into thirds: 1/3 for marinating, 1/3 for basting, and 1/3 for topping.

Rosemary Lemon Grilled Chicken is a flavorful and aromatic dish that combines the zesty brightness of fresh lemon with the earthy, pine-like essence of rosemary. This recipe features tender chicken infused with a simple yet vibrant marinade, then grilled to perfection for a juicy and delicious result. Ideal for a light summer meal or a quick weeknight dinner, it pairs beautifully with seasonal vegetables or a fresh salad, offering a healthy and satisfying option that’s sure to impress.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup butter
  • ½ cup fresh rosemary
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 lemon, zested
  • ¼ cup fresh lemon juice
  • 6 (6 ounce) skinless, boneless chicken breast halves
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps:

  1. In a food processor, blend butter, rosemary, garlic, lemon zest, and lemon juice together.
  2. Pour 1/3 of the blended mixture into a small bowl for marinade. Cover remaining mixture, and set aside.
  3. Lightly season chicken breasts with salt and pepper. Rub chicken breasts with marinade.
  4. Place chicken breasts on a platter, cover, and refrigerate for 3 hours.
  5. Preheat an outdoor grill for high heat and lightly oil the grate.
  6. Transfer half of the reserved rosemary and lemon mixture into a bowl for basting. Cover remaining mixture, and set aside for topping cooked chicken.
  7. Cook chicken breasts on hot grill, basting with rosemary and lemon basting mixture, about 4 minutes per side.
  8. An instant-read thermometer inserted into the center should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).
  9. Remove chicken breasts from the grill, and top with remaining rosemary and lemon mixture.
